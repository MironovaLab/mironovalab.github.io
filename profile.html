Plant Developmental Regulomics

Profile

Victoria Mironova is an Associate Professor in the Department of Plant and Animal Biology at Radboud University (RIBES). Her research focuses on computational plant developmental biology, with particular emphasis on molecular, cellular, and physiological responses to abiotic stresses.

A central objective of the group is to integrate single-cell transcriptomics, genomics, and mathematical modelling approaches to uncover self-organization principles in plant tissues. The lab operates across dry-lab and wet-lab modalities, develops and maintains open-source analytical resources, and collaborates closely with academic and industrial partners.

The group teaches across BSc-, MSc-, and PhD-level courses in plant genomics, computational biology, and single-cell analysis, and supervises internship projects and research-based training in high-dimensional data analysis, imaging-integrated workflows, and manuscript preparation.
